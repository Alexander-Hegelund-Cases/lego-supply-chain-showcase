This glossary defines core terminology, ontologies, and concepts utilised within the LEGO Enterprise Knowledge Platform (EKP) strategic prototype.

### EKP (Enterprise Knowledge Platform)
An active semantic layer that breaks down data silos, enabling cross-functional teams to query complex, multi-dimensional relationships (e.g., Supply Chain, ESG, Finance) in real-time to drive decision intelligence. 

### FAIR(EST) Data Principles
<a name="fairest"></a>
An expansion of the foundational FAIR(EST) framework (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable), adding **E**xtensible, **S**ecure, and **T**rustworthy. In the context of a Knowledge Graph, this ensures organizational data becomes a scalable asset rather than an isolated liability. 
*🔗 Authoritative Source: [Making Data FAIR(EST) - Flur.ee](https://flur.ee/fluree-blog/making-data-fairest/)*

### SCOR-DS (Supply Chain Operations Reference - Digital Standard)
<a name="scor-ds"></a>
A cross-industry standard methodology for supply chain management. This prototype maps its semantic nodes directly to SCOR-DS v14.0 components (Source, Make, Deliver) to ensure enterprise-grade interoperability.
*🔗 Authoritative Source: [ASCM SCOR Digital Standard](https://www.ascm.org/corporate-transformation/standards-tools/scor-ds/)*

### SPOF (Single Point of Failure)
<a name="spof"></a>
A critical node in the supply chain dependency graph (e.g., a Tier-2 supplier producing a unique material) where disruption would cascade unpredictably across the network and halt downstream production.
*🔗 Authoritative Source: [Gartner: Supply Chain Risk Management](https://www.gartner.com/en/supply-chain/insights/supply-chain-risk-management)*

### Mass Balance Accounting (ISCC PLUS)
<a name="mass-balance"></a>
A chain-of-custody methodology tracking sustainable materials through complex supply chains. This graph uses it to map certified 'Bio-PE' (bio-polyethylene) resin offsets from upstream suppliers down to specific high-visibility product themes. 
*🔗 Authoritative Source: [ISCC System: Mass Balance Approach](https://www.iscc-system.org/about/mass-balance/)*

### Knowledge Graph
A structured network of semantic data (entities and relationships) allowing machines and humans to extract deep contextual insights, visualize dependencies, and perform complex scenario simulations.
*🔗 Authoritative Source: [Neo4j: What is a Knowledge Graph?](https://neo4j.com/knowledge-graphs/)*

### Semantic Layer
A business representation of underlying complex data that helps end users to access data autonomously, using common business terms, shielding them from the underlying complexity of the database schemas.
*🔗 Authoritative Source: [Gartner Glossary: Semantic Data Model](https://www.gartner.com/en/information-technology/glossary/semantic-data-model)*
